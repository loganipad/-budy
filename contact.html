Before you hit send

A little context up front usually makes the first reply useful instead of just asking for basic details.

1
Use the account emailTell us which email you used for login or checkout so we can find the right account and subscription record.
2
Name the exact issueSay what broke, what page or flow it happened on, and whether the problem is billing, access, score history, or guarantee-related.
3
Add timing or proofIf the issue is visual, billing-related, or tied to a specific test result, include a screenshot and the approximate date or purchase time.
Visual or payment issue?Screenshots, checkout timing, and the exact email used at purchase are the fastest way to reduce back-and-forth.

Common request types

These are the issues that usually benefit from a little extra context in the first email.

Missing premium access

Premium is not showing up, the plan status looks wrong, or access did not update after checkout.

Include: the email used at checkout and the approximate purchase time.

Wrong score or test-history issue

A result looks off, a score is missing, or a test attempt did not save the way you expected.

Include: the section, approximate date, and what result you expected to see.

Refund or guarantee review

You need clarification on program terms, guarantee status, or a support review tied to a qualifying plan.

Include: the plan used, study period, and any qualifying score evidence relevant to the request.
